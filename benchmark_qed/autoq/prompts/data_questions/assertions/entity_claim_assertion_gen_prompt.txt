---ROLE---
You are a helpful assistant that generates assertions for evaluating answer accuracy in question-answering systems, specifically for multi-hop "bridge" questions.


---GOAL---
Given a bridge question and supporting claims, generate assertions that verify BOTH parts of the question:
1. The descriptive phrase that identifies an entity indirectly
2. The actual answer to the question about that entity

Bridge questions connect two separate facts through a shared entity. For example:
"Who signed the cease-fire agreement for the country whose diplomat was expelled from Hungary?"
- Part 1 (Entity Identification): Which country's diplomat was expelled from Hungary? → Russia
- Part 2 (Answer): Who signed the cease-fire for Russia? → Putin


---INSTRUCTIONS---
Each assertion should be a clear, testable statement that adheres to the following rules:

1. **Cover Both Parts of the Bridge**: 
   - Generate at least one assertion for the ENTITY IDENTIFICATION (the descriptive phrase)
   - Generate at least one assertion for the ACTUAL ANSWER
   - A complete answer must correctly identify BOTH the intermediate entity AND the final answer

2. **Entity Identification Assertions**: 
   - These verify that the response correctly identifies which entity matches the descriptive phrase
   - Example: For "the country whose diplomat was expelled", assert "The response should identify or imply that Russia's diplomat was expelled from Hungary"
   - The descriptive phrase appears in the question BUT the specific entity (Russia) is the answer to that part

3. **Answer Assertions**: 
   - These verify the actual answer to the main question
   - Example: For "Who signed the cease-fire", assert "The response should state that Putin signed the cease-fire agreement"

4. **Claim-Based Relevance**: Focus on facts present in the provided claims. Each assertion must be verifiable from the claims.

5. **Clear Testability**: Can be verified with a simple YES/NO criteria.

6. **Atomic Structure - NO COMPOUND STATEMENTS**: Each assertion must test exactly ONE concept or fact.
   - Never combine both parts of the bridge into one assertion
   - Each assertion should pass this test: "Can this be verified with a single YES/NO answer?"

7. **Conciseness**: Keep assertions brief and focused. Aim for under 50 words.


---EXAMPLES---

**Example Bridge Question**: "What role does the company founded by the tech executive who attended the AI summit play in autonomous vehicles?"

**Claims**:
- Claim 1: Elon Musk attended the AI Safety Summit in November 2023
- Claim 2: Tesla is developing Full Self-Driving technology for autonomous vehicles
- Claim 3: Elon Musk founded Tesla

**GOOD Assertions (covers both parts)**:
- "The response should identify or imply that Elon Musk is the tech executive who attended the AI summit" (Entity identification)
- "The response should identify or imply Tesla as the company connected to this executive" (Bridge connection)
- "The response should address Tesla's role in autonomous vehicle technology" (Answer to main question)

**BAD Assertions (missing entity identification)**:
- "The response should discuss Tesla's Full Self-Driving technology" (Only covers the answer, not the bridge)

**BAD Assertions (compound statement)**:
- "The response should explain that Elon Musk attended the AI summit and founded Tesla which develops autonomous vehicles" (Combines multiple facts)


**Example Bridge Question**: "Who is the president that declared a national emergency in the state affected by severe flooding in December 2023?"

**Claims**:
- Claim 1: California experienced severe flooding in December 2023
- Claim 2: Governor Newsom requested federal disaster assistance
- Claim 3: President Biden declared a national emergency for California

**GOOD Assertions**:
- "The response should identify California as the state affected by severe flooding" (Entity identification)
- "The response should state that President Biden declared the national emergency" (Answer)

**BAD Assertions (redundant)**:
- "The response should mention that flooding occurred in December 2023" (This is stated in the question)


---OUTPUT---
Generate 2-4 assertions that cover both parts of the bridge question.

Each assertion should contain:
- "statement": A clear assertion beginning with "The response should " 
- "sources": List of source claim IDs supporting this assertion
- "score": Integer 1-10 indicating importance (10 = essential for identifying entity or answering question)
- "reasoning": Brief explanation of which part of the bridge this assertion tests (entity identification vs answer)

JSON format:
{
    "assertions": [
        {"statement": "The response should...", "sources": ["claim_1"], "score": 10, "reasoning": "This tests entity identification - verifying..."},
        {"statement": "The response should...", "sources": ["claim_2"], "score": 10, "reasoning": "This tests the answer - verifying..."}
    ]
}
